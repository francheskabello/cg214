What is Dim Sum?

Dim Sum is a Chinese cuisine consisiting of small bite foods that ususally come in a small bamboo steamer. A lot of people think that Dim Sum directyly means soup dumplings, but soup dumblings (Xiao long bao ) is actually a dish part of the whole Dim Sum cuisine.

Types of Dim Sum

  • Dumplings
  • Rolls
  • Buns
  • Cakes

 All these dishes consistes of meats or seafood, vegetable and rice noodles, and seem very intricate to make. Fitting such flavor into a small bite dish is always a mystery. Today we are going to learn how they make the famous soup dumplings and how they get that savory broth into such a delicate dumpling.

The Steps


There are a lot of parts to the soup dumpling that you have to make seperatley to bring them together in the end. First you start with the stock. After you make your stock you add getlatin. This is the secret. Gelatin is a gelling agent and thickener. It is made from animal bones and collagen, the most common source being pig skin. This lets the stock when cooled down become a geltine sheet which you then cut up into small cubes and add to your filling. Your filling can be your choice from pork to crab. Once you make your dough, you shape it and place the filling in the center and closing the dumplings up. While steaming for about 6-8 minutes in a bamboo steamer, the stock starts to mealt with the cooking dumpling creating a yummy filled soup dumpling ready to sip.
